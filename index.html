The Roast

Four stages, one decision each.

A roast is a short window with no do-overs. This is the sequence we read the bean against, lot after lot.

01 / Charge 180°C

Green in

Beans hit the drum. We log moisture and density to set the charge temperature for that specific lot.

02 / Turning point 92°C

Bean bottoms out

The lowest temperature the mass reaches before it starts climbing again — our anchor for the whole curve.

03 / First crack 196°C

Sugars break

An audible signal. From here, development time decides how the acidity and body will read in the cup.

04 / Drop 205°C

Out and cooling

Beans hit the cooling tray within seconds. Every minute past this point changes the roast.

Brew Guide

Dial it in at home.

Ratios we test every lot against before it ships. Start here, then adjust to taste.

V60

20g coffee, 320g water at 94°C. Bloom 45s, pour in three stages, done by 2:45.

1:15

AeroPress

17g coffee, 255g water. Steep 1:30 inverted, gentle stir, press over 30s.

1:17

French Press

30g coffee, 510g water at 96°C. Steep 4 minutes, break the crust, plunge.

Espresso
